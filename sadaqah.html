How Funds Are Disbursed
Every donation collected through SupportKori is held separately and disbursed in tranches to individual entrepreneurs — not pooled into a general fund.

  • ✓ In tranches of ৳5,000–20,000 per entrepreneur

  • ✓ No overhead deducted — 0% management cut from donations
  • ✓ SupportKori platform fee (if any) disclosed upfront

Questions Answered

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to feel confident before giving.

Does my donation count as Zakat? +
This initiative is structured as Sadaqah (voluntary charity), not formally as Zakat. However, since funds go directly to individual entrepreneurs who are below the nisab threshold, many scholars would consider this valid as Zakat too. We recommend consulting your own scholar. If your intention is Zakat, please ensure you're satisfied that recipients qualify under nisab rules. Either way, the impact is identical.
Why "Sadaqah" instead of "Zakat"? +
Branding this as Zakat would require us to verify the nisab eligibility of every individual recipient — a compliance burden we cannot do at scale for 100+ founders. By framing it as Sadaqah, we can accept donations from everyone (Zakat-intending or not), ensure maximum participation, and avoid inadvertently invalidating anyone's Zakat due to unverified eligibility. It is the more cautious and inclusive approach.
How do I know the money actually reaches founders? +
We publish monthly disbursement reports on LinkedIn with names (or initials if privacy is requested), business types, and amounts given. At the end of Ramadan, a full transparency report will be shared publicly and emailed to all SupportKori donors. We treat this with the same rigor we apply to investor reporting.
What if the founder misuses the money? +
All selected founders are vetted through Capallo's existing network and agree to monthly check-ins. In the event of misuse, Capallo will document it, pursue recovery where legally possible, and report transparently to donors. We cannot guarantee outcomes — but we guarantee transparency and accountability, which is the Islamic standard for those who handle others' amanah.
Is this connected to Capallo's investor deals? +
No. The Sadaqah fund and Capallo's investment deals are completely separate. Investor capital is never mixed with charity funds. The only connection is that Capallo donates 100% of its own management fees from Ramadan deals to this fund — but that is Capallo's own money, not investors' money.
Who decides which entrepreneurs receive the funds? +
Capallo curates the list, drawing from founders who've applied to Capallo for working capital, referrals from the Capallo network, and community nominations. Selection criteria: genuine financial need, viable business idea, and willingness to provide progress updates. Priority given to women-led and marginalized founders.
Can I donate a specific amount for one entrepreneur? +
Yes — if you donate ৳10,000–20,000 and specify in the notes that you'd like it directed to a single founder, we'll do our best to honour that. For amounts under ৳5,000, funds are pooled and distributed collectively to maximise impact. Reach out to saleh.sazzad@capallo.co or WP (+88 01645 823853) for large or directed donations.
What happens to unspent funds? +
Any unspent funds at the end of the Ramadan cycle will be carried forward to the next disbursement round and disclosed in the transparency report. No funds will be used for Capallo's operating expenses. If you'd prefer a refund of undeployed funds, contact us within 30 days of the end of Ramadan.
⚠️ Important: This is a charitable giving initiative, not an investment. You will not receive financial returns on your donation. Capallo acts as a curator and distributor, not a financial institution. This initiative is not regulated by any financial authority in Bangladesh. All disbursements are acts of charitable giving, and Capallo volunteers its time and infrastructure at no cost to donors.
