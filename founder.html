THE ORIGIN STORY

Why Capallo Exists

During a startup showcase at my previous role at a local angel investing network, I witnessed something that changed the trajectory of my career‚Äîand ultimately, led to Capallo's creation.

An investor approached me, visibly frustrated. He hadn't received a single update from the startup he'd invested in. No financials. No progress reports. Nothing. When he tried to exit, the founder was sympathetic but honest: "Our cashflow won't allow a buyback. The only way out is if you find a buyer yourself."

"I gotta build this. Or else the private market venture investing ecosystem won't develop in Bangladesh."

That moment crystallized a harsh reality: In a frontier market like Bangladesh with fewer than 200 venture investors, finding secondary buyers is nearly impossible. The ecosystem was broken‚Äînot because of bad founders or unwilling investors, but because we lacked the infrastructure, education, and trust mechanisms that mature markets take for granted.

I realized that to create exits, we first needed to create investors. Lots of them. We needed to make venture equity a mainstream asset class. Only then could we build the liquidity that makes exits possible.

That's when Capallo was born‚Äînot just as a platform, but as a mission.

I started with investor education through Capallo Venture School, with an ambitious goal: create 10,000 venture investors in the next 5 years. But education alone wasn't enough. I needed quality deal flow. So I built comprehensive founder services‚Äîfrom investment advisory to our Business-in-a-Box offering‚Äîto identify and support the 100 most promising, high-cashflow companies in Bangladesh.

The name "Capallo" comes from capital allocation‚Äîbecause that's exactly what Bangladesh needs: a structured, protected way for people to allocate their hard-earned money into innovation and societal value creation. To take our country one step further.
